# Mythos — Puddle

Ribbon made sound you played at. Puddle makes sound you fall into.

The fork happened when the question changed — from *what can I play?* to *what can I leave running?* There's a mode of listening that isn't performance. Ambient. Generative. Something in the room that breathes on its own and doesn't demand attention.

Puddle is ribbon released from the player. The same audio engine, the same browser-native stack, but the interaction model inverted. You set a condition and step back. The thing continues. It knows nothing about what you need to do next, and that's the point.

The name came naturally. A ribbon of water, interrupted, becomes a puddle — still, reflective, its own thing. It remembers where it came from but it doesn't move the same way.

If ribbon is the performance, puddle is what plays while the performer rests.
